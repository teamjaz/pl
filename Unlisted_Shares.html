Unlisted Shares




Unlisted Securities are shares, debentures or any other securities that are not traded on an exchange but through the over-the-counter (OTC) market. Unlisted securities are also called OTC securities

Market makers facilitate the buying and selling of unlisted securities in the OTC market. Because they are not exchange traded, unlisted securities can be less liquid than listed securities

Here’s what you need to know about unlisted shares

  • 1
  • Unlisted companies are acquired by private investors like founders, founders’ family and peers. Listed companies on the other hand are acquired by several shareholders
  • 2
  • There is no openly available market for shares; therefore, they are illiquid. Shares on the other hand are easily tradable on the stock exchange
  • 3
  • Valuation of unlisted shares is sometimes uncertain while shares traded on an exchange have their price information available upfront
  • 4
  • Regulatory requirements are less stringent about unlisted shares
  • 1
  • Taxation on Unlisted Shares

    • Unlisted Stock is not registered on any approved stock exchange. Therefore, the Organization will not pay STT, i.e. Securities Tax on these shares. The retention time is 24 months.

  • 1
  • Long Term Capital Gain (LTCG)

    • The Long Term Capital Gain (LTCG) or Long Term Capital Loss (LTCL) is the gain or loss on such transactions if an investor sells an unlisted stock owned for more than 24 months.

  • 1
  • Short Term Capital Gain (STCG)

    • When an owner sells an unlisted stock owned for up to 24 months, a short term capital gain (STCG) or short term capital loss (STCL) is a gain or loss on such a transaction.
